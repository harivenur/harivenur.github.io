Journalism

2005-2007

India
Print Media

English Newspapers, Delhi

I started my journalism career as a Sub-editor with The Financial Express (FE), the business newspaper of the Indian Express group, in New Delhi. Though my primary job at FE was mostly editing news stories, making newspaper pages, giving headlines, and captions, etc., I also wrote a few stories for them, including front-page items. Later, I joined the launch team of HT Media's joint venture with the The Wall Street Journal, called Mint, as a Copy Editor, with the same job profile sans reporting. Realizing that biz-journalism in Delhi was not quite the fun I needed, I accepted a Yale scholarship to start my career in ecology and conservation.

2008-2010

USA
Online Media

Yale and New York

Though I never returned to being a full-time journalist, I continued to satisfy my journalistic itch by working with online publications. For over two years, I worked as a Research Assistant and Editorial Intern with Yale Environment 360 (E360), as it was starting to establish under the able editorial leadership of Roger Cohn, the former editor of Mother Jones and Audubon Magazine. My primary job at E360 was to fact-check news stories written by various professional journalists and authors, and help them with additional research. Though I never wrote for E360, I was lucky enough to work with excellent writers such as David Quammen, Fred Pearce, Richard Conniff, etc. After E360, I had a short stint with the Audubon Magazine, New York, where I fact-checked stories and managed to get one story published.
