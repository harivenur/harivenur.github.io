About me

I am a tropical plant community ecologist. I defended my PhD (2022) at the Yale University (New Haven, CT, USA), where I studied herbaceous plant diversity variation in seasonal tropical forests. I am professionally trained, skilled and experienced in four fields: tropical forest ecology, wildlife conservation, agriculture, and journalism. My repertoire was built through years of focussed work in a direction that helped me make more sense of the world. For almost a decade, I worked with non-governmental conservation agencies, activist groups and professional media houses in the US and India. A major part of this work has been on wildlife conservation projects throughout India, Latin America and the Caribbean, Africa, and South East Asia, mostly on animals and landscapes. My academic research focuses on tropical forest plants, on which I have worked in wet and dry forests in South America and India.

After submitting my PhD dissertation, I look forward to working more on the ecology of tropical forest plants, especially, their diversity, biotic and abiotic interactions, and how they make sense to the human world.

Explore this simple website to learn about my work and ideas. If you need to know more, if you would like to work with me, or if you have any suggestions, please email me!
