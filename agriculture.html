Agriculture

4 Years

BSc. Agriculture
Bachelor's study and training

I completed my Bachelor's (Science) in Agriculture from the College of Agriculture, Vellayani, Thiruvananthapuram, one of the campuses of the Kerala Agricultural University (KAU). The students were rigorously trained in a range of classes, from biology courses such as Botany, Plant taxonomy, Entomology, Biochemistry, Analytical Chemistry, and Microbiology to applied courses including Agronomy, Horticulture, Soil Science, Animal Husbandry, and Biotechnology, along with many supportive courses such as Economics, Social sciences, Agricultural Extension, and Farm Engineering. The program also mandated six months of extensive rural work, where the students received direct work experience at government agricultural extension centers (Krishi Bhavans), local-self governments (Panchayat), and private farms. The impact of the BSc. program surprisingly seem to last forever. Throughout my professional and academic life, I often find my KAU training constantly and consistently helping me see the forest for the trees, thus giving me an edge over people from other disciplines,.

1 Year

Independent Work
Personal Travel, Research and Writing

By the end of my formal (BSc.) studies in Agriculture, I had become disappointed with chemical-based farming, and the collapse of the farming ecosystems in the state of Kerala. Though I wanted to go for a Masters study, some health issues sort of put a temporary hold on my hopes. For almost a year, as and when health allowed, I travelled throughout Kerala and Tamil Nadu, and met with people practicing alternative farming practices such as those influenced by Masanobu Fukuoka and Bill Mollison; farmers trying to save traditional crop varieties, cattle breeds, and farming systems; and activists working on a range of farm-related and environmental subjects. I also met with people working on other agriculture-related sustainable practices such as pottery and bamboo-based products, and on alternative education. With the help of a local birding group, I also learned the basics of bird-watching, mostly in farm ecosystems and protected forests. A main goal of my travels was to learn about alternative farming methods, and understand how Kerala's rice-farming systems collapsed since the 1970's. In the process, I familiarized myself with agricultural and natural landscapes of Kerala and Southern Tamil Nadu, and compiled information on plant-based DIY pesticides, fungicides and bactericides that could be used in agriculture and animal husbandry, and experimented with new combinations of such concoctions with the help of farmers. During the same period, I also started my career as a freelance journalist, and managed to get a couple of my articles on agriculture published. All my travel was supported by my parents during the period.

2 Years

Non-governmental Organization
Grassroot work as an agricultural researcher and activist

I ended my independent research on farm issues by joining a local non-governmental activist and research group called Thanal. I worked with Thanal for about two years as a research associate. Most of my work was related to two things: (1) Kickstarting the group's "Save Our Rice" project, a campaign aimed to conserve and sustain traditional rice farming, varities, seeds, and farm ecosystems. Along with another researcher, I did most of the initial legwork to set the ball moving, and now I learn that it has grown into a large collaborative project; (2) Kickstarting the group's second major project, Organic Bazaar, a local organic-marketing initiative, perhaps the first of its kind in Kerala. I formulated the original standards for the 'organic' produce for the market, and helped in evaluation, while another person handled the marketing aspect. To read more about the initial phase, please download this. At Thanal, I also worked as a resource person and activist for Thanal's then flag-ship "Zero Waste" project. I was also part of two research projects one on pesticide poisoning, and the other an investigation on POABS, a private company that used to ran the Thiruvananthapuram City's waste treatment plant, which had cause massive pollution in a the village it was located. Another major work was the regular monitoring of birds in the Vellayani fresh-water lake ecosystem in Thiruvananthapuram.
