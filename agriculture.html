Agriculture
Bachelor's study and training

I completed my Bachelor's (Science) in Agriculture from the College of Agriculture, Vellayani, of the Kerala Agricultural University (KAU). I was trained in a range of subjects, including science (e.g., Botany and plant taxonomy, Entomology, Biochemistry, Analytical Chemistry, Microbiology, etc.), application (e.g., Agronomy, Horticulture, Pathology, Soil Science, Animal Husbandry, Biotechnology, etc.), and supportive courses (e.g., Economics, Social sciences, Farm Engineering, etc.). I also completed six months of rural extension work at government agricultural extension centers, local-self governments, and private farms. My KAU training have always helped me see the forest for the trees, and has me an edge over people from other disciplines.

Personal Travel, Research and Writing

Disillusioned and disappointed with the lethal stranglehold of private-corporation-dependent and chemical-based farming ideology within Indian farm education and politics, and alarmed by the collapse of the agroecosystems in Kerala, I travelled throughout Western Ghats, Deccan, and southern plains to study sustainable farming practices. I met with scores of farmers and activists trying to save traditional crop varieties, cattle breeds, farming systems, and knowledge. I compiled information on natural DIY pesticides, fungicides and bactericides, and experimented with new combinations with the help of individual farmers and private farms. I also conducted bird monitoring in local agroecosystems and protected forests, and wrote (freelance) in popular Malayalam magazines, during the period.

Grassroot work as an agricultural researcher and activist

Ending my independent research, I joined a local non-governmental group called Thanal. I primarily worked on kickstarting two of the group's flag-ship projects, (1) Save Our Rice campaign, aimed to conserve and sustain traditional rice farming, varities, seeds, and farm ecosystems; and (2) Organic Bazaar, a local organic-marketing initiative, the first of its kind in Kerala. I formulated the original organic-standards for the market, and helped in farm inspections. I also worked as a resource person for a solid-waste-management initiative called "Zero Waste Project", and assisted in a research project on pesticide poisoning.
